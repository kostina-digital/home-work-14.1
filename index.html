Magnus Carlsen continues to redefine chess with his victory in the innovative Freestyle format in Paris.​ Magnus Carlsen secured a commanding victory at the Paris Freestyle Chess Grand Slam, showcasing his adaptability and strategic prowess in this novel format that blends elements of classical, rapid, and Chess960 styles. The tournament, held at the Pavillon Chesnaie du Roy, featured a randomized starting position, eliminating traditional opening preparations and emphasizing creativity and on-the-spot thinking. Carlsen triumphed over a strong field, including a 1.5–0.5 win against Hikaru Nakamura in the final, earning a $200,000 prize and leading the Grand Slam standings with 40 points. This victory reinforces Carlsen's status as one of the game's most versatile players and highlights the growing popularity of Freestyle Chess, with upcoming events scheduled in Las Vegas, New Delhi, and Cape Town.

Chess experiences a cultural renaissance in the UK, fueled by media and fashion influences.​ The UK is witnessing a significant chess revival, driven by popular media and social media influencers. Television shows like Netflix's "The Queen’s Gambit" and BBC2’s "Chess Masters: The Endgame" have sparked renewed interest in the game. Retailers report substantial increases in chess set sales, with Selfridges noting a 260% annual rise. Fashion brands such as Burberry and Daily Paper are incorporating chess motifs into their collections, reflecting the game's aesthetic appeal. Online platforms like Chess.com have seen a surge in UK users, now ranking third globally. Influencers like Andrea Botez and Levy Rozman (GothamChess) have further popularized chess among younger audiences, contributing to its growing cultural significance.

FIDE launches a global initiative to harness chess for social good in 2025.​ The International Chess Federation (FIDE) has designated 2025 as the "Year of Social Chess," aiming to utilize the game as a tool for social improvement. The initiative focuses on bringing chess to diverse communities and settings, including prisons, refugee camps, elderly care homes, and addiction recovery centers. Key events include the "Chess for Freedom Conference" in Georgia, highlighting the role of chess in prison rehabilitation programs, and an Intercontinental Online Championship for prisoners. FIDE President Arkady Dvorkovich emphasized the goal of making chess a force for positive change, connecting people worldwide and making a real difference in their lives.
